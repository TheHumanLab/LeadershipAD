What this diagnostic does

This is not a leadership
development assessment.

It does not evaluate character, style, or leadership potential. It does not measure how well-liked a leader is, or how aligned the team feels.

The Leadership Authority Diagnostic examines authority mechanics — the structural patterns of decision-making, control, escalation, and power concentration that determine whether an organisation can perform and scale independently of the individuals currently leading it.

Leadership failure rarely begins with character. It begins with structural misconfiguration — authority placed in the wrong location, delegation that exists formally but not operationally, and decision patterns that gradually destabilise the system they are meant to lead.

This diagnostic surfaces that pattern. In 28 questions. Before it costs you.

Diagnostic dimensions

Six structural
dimensions examined.

Each dimension measures both stated perception and revealed behaviour — the gap between the two is where the diagnostic intelligence lives.

01
Decision Rights Architecture
Whether decision authority is clearly defined and positioned at the correct level — or structurally ambiguous.
02
Authority–Role Alignment
Whether managers are genuinely empowered within their scope, or whether formal authority and operational reality diverge.
03
Load Distribution & Compensation
Whether leadership decision burden is sustainable — or whether specific individuals are absorbing structural weakness through personal effort.
04
Escalation & Information Integrity
Whether escalation channels are trusted and functioning — or whether information is withheld, suppressed, or arriving too late.
05
Power Concentration & Network Dependency
Whether influence flows through formal structures — or whether informal networks have become load-bearing.
06
Succession Depth & Structural Dependency
Whether the organisation can sustain itself without key individuals — or whether it carries unacknowledged single-point-of-failure risk.
